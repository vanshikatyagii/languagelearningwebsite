Nouns

Nouns are words that name people, places, things, and ideas. Like: People: John, teacher, family Places: school, park, India Things: book, pen, computer Ideas: love, happiness, time Nouns can be counted (one book, two pens) or uncountable (water, air, music)

Pronouns

Pronouns are words used to replace nouns, making sentences clearer and more concise. Common pronouns include "he," "she," "it," "they," and "we." Learning pronouns is essential in English as they help avoid repetition and maintain fluency in communication.

Verbs

Verbs are the "doing" words in a sentence. They tell us what someone or something is doing, feeling, or being. Think of them as action words like "walk," "eat," "sleep," or "think." Verbs can also describe states like "is," "seems," or "feels." They change form to show different times (past, present, future) and who is doing the action.
